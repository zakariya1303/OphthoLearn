Module 1: How to take an ophthalmology history

A concise, structured history for common and high-risk eye presentations.

  • Onset: Sudden vs gradual, unilateral vs bilateral.
  • Pain: Severity, associated headache, nausea/vomiting.
  • Visual symptoms: Blurring, distortion, flashes/floaters, “curtain” over vision, diplopia.
  • Red flags: Reduced visual acuity, severe pain, trauma, contact lens use with red eye.
  • Ocular history: Previous surgery, glaucoma, diabetic eye disease, prior episodes.
  • Systemic history: Vascular risk factors, autoimmune symptoms, temporal headache/jaw claudication.
  • Medication/allergy: Especially steroids/anticoagulants and drug allergies.

Tip: Always summarise and state your differential + urgency at the end.
