Cranial Nerve & Eye OSCE Station

Use this as a script for an OSCE station. Each step can be expanded with prompts you can say out loud.

5–7 minute station

You are the FY1 doctor asked to perform a focused cranial nerve and eye examination on a standardised patient complaining of blurred vision.

Examiner prompt: “Please perform a cranial nerve and eye examination. You do not need to examine other systems. Explain your findings as you go.”

OSCE timer: 07:00

Use this timer to practise under exam conditions. It runs only on your device and does not record any data.

  • Wash or gel hands, introduce yourself, confirm patient name and DOB.
  • Explain the exam in simple terms and gain consent.
  • Check for pain, ask about comfort, ensure lighting and positioning are appropriate.
  • Snellen chart at 6m (or 3m) with glasses if worn.
  • Test each eye separately, then both together.
  • State how you would record the findings correctly.
  • Confrontation testing in all quadrants.
  • Ask if any areas of vision appear “missing” or “blurred”.
  • Mention fundoscopy and key optic disc findings.
  • Inspect pupil size, shape and equality.
  • Test direct and consensual light reflexes.
  • Perform swinging light test for RAPD and interpret.
  • Ask about diplopia before starting.
  • Perform H-test, ensuring head remains still.
  • Comment on nystagmus and any movement limitation.
  • Light touch sensation over V1, V2, V3 (if time allows).
  • Test facial movements: eyebrows, eye closure, cheek puff, smile.
  • Mention corneal reflex and its afferent/efferent limbs.
  • State additional tests: fundoscopy, IOP measurement, colour vision.
  • Summarise key findings clearly and concisely.
  • Suggest likely diagnoses and appropriate next steps/referrals.
